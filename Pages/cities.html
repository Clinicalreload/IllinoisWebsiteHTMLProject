Springfield

The state Capital of Illinois is Springfield. According to Google, here is where Abraham Lincoln's coffin is located. Lincoln is known for the adoption of the Emancipation Proclamation which ultimately set forth the movement to free the slaves as well as his famous Gettysburg Address speech. According to Google, the estimated population of Springfield is 116,250 people. Springfield was incorporated in year 1832 located in central Illinois. Springfield is known as an urban city with above average income levels for the residents of the town.

Attractions

Some of the must-visit attractions of Springfield include:

  1. Abraham Lincoln Presidential Library and Museum
  2. Lincoln Home National Historic Site
  3. Dana-Thomas House
  4. Lincoln Tomb
  5. Old State Capitol State Historic Site
  6. Illinois State Capitol
